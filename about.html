Our story

A hundred years of hikers, and counting.

The Wisconsin Go Hiking Club was founded in 1924 by a small group of Milwaukee schoolteachers who wanted to spend their Sundays in the kettle moraine instead of the parlor. They built tradition; we keep it walking.

Mission

Boots on trails. Hands on conservation.

We exist to help every Wisconsinite — beginner or thru-hiker — find a way onto a trail, and to keep those trails open for the next hundred years.

The club operates entirely through volunteer effort. Trip leaders plan weekly outings on the Ice Age Trail, the North Country Trail, the state's recreation trails, and our many state parks and forests. Members log thousands of trail-maintenance hours each year through partnerships with the Ice Age Trail Alliance, the Wisconsin DNR, and the U.S. Forest Service.

We're proud of three things: that a curious newcomer can show up at any group hike and feel welcome; that our trip records go back to 1924; and that nearly every mile we walk, we've helped keep walkable.

Our history

A timeline.

1924

The club is founded

Seven Milwaukee teachers organize a "Sunday Walking Society." First recorded hike: the Menomonee River Parkway, eight miles round-trip.

1958

The Kettle Moraine project

Members help survey what will become the Ice Age Trail's Kettle Moraine segments — three decades before federal designation.

1980

National Scenic Trails era

Congress designates the Ice Age Trail and North Country Trail. WGHC trip leaders walk every Wisconsin mile in the year that follows.

1995

The Trail Stewardship Pact

Formal partnership with the Ice Age Trail Alliance and DNR. WGHC adopts six segments for ongoing maintenance.

2024

Centennial year

One hundred years of hikes. We launched the digital Trail Finder, mapping every segment our trip leaders have walked since 1924.
